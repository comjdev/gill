In-home newborn sessions bring the photography to you, so you can settle into the experience in your own space, with your rhythms, and without the stress of packing bags or beating the Melbourne traffic.

This guide walks you through how in-home newborn photography works, why it is safe, what is included, and how we bring siblings and pets into the story. You will also find gentle answers to common questions about timing and whether you might regret skipping newborn photos.

## What makes an in-home session calm and safe

Safety is the foundation of every newborn session. That starts with baby-led pacing. Your little one sets the tempo. We pause for feeds, cuddles, nappy changes, and settling whenever you need. There is no rush, no stopwatch, and no forcing a pose that does not feel natural.

I work with soft, natural light and relaxed positions that keep baby supported at all times. Most images happen in your arms, on your bed, or in a cosy bassinet with you right next to your baby. I do not use complicated props or risky set-ups. Instead, I rely on gentle guidance and angles that flatter without putting baby in uncomfortable positions.

Your home is also a safer environment for temperature and hygiene. In early summer, when Melbourne days can turn hot quickly, we can create a comfortable room temperature, draw the blinds for soft light, and keep baby in familiar clothing or wraps. No overheating, no moving between locations, and no exposure to crowded spaces during a season of holiday gatherings.

If you are wondering, is a newborn photoshoot safe, the answer is yes when it is baby-led, minimally posed, and guided by a photographer who puts comfort first. I have photographed many new families across Melbourne, and parents often tell me the session felt like a calm morning at home with a friend who happens to have a camera.

## A baby-led flow from hello to goodbye

Every session begins with a chat while you settle in. I will have a quick look for the best pockets of light in your home, often near a window in the lounge or bedroom. We start with the simplest photos, usually cuddles on the couch or skin to skin moments on the bed, then follow your baby's cues from there.

If baby needs a feed, we pause. If a nappy needs changing, we pause. Those in-between moments often become favourite images, like a hand resting on your shoulder or the sleepy milk-drunk yawn after a feed. There is no expectation for quiet perfection. Your real life is the story.

## Siblings, pets, and the sweet chaos of family

Big brothers and sisters often make the session. I like to invite siblings in for the first part while their energy is highest, then give them breaks to play or snack. We keep prompts simple, like asking them to count baby's toes or tell a secret to their new teammate. If a cuddle turns into a giggle fit, we roll with it.

Pets are welcome. A gentle paw near the bassinet or a curious nose sniffing tiny feet can be a gorgeous part of your family story. Safety always comes first. We only include pets when you are comfortable, and we keep you within arm's reach.

## Timing your newborn photos

How soon after birth should you get newborn photos, and how old should a newborn be for a photoshoot? There is no single right answer. For the classic sleepy, curled images, many families choose the first two weeks. That said, in-home lifestyle sessions are flexible. Three to six weeks can be beautiful too, with more awake gazes and stretches. If your recovery takes priority, or if life simply gets busy in the early-summer holiday season, you have not missed your chance. Your baby will keep changing, and every stage is worth remembering.

If you want the option for those very early snuggly images during December or January, consider pencilling a window into your calendar before baby arrives. As a melbourne photographer, I hold space for early arrivals and rescheduling so we can match your baby's timing.

## What to wear and how to prepare, without overthinking it

Choose simple, soft clothing in neutral or light tones that reflect your home. Avoid heavy patterns that distract from faces. For baby, a comfy onesie, a wrap, or even a nappy and singlet is perfect. I bring light wraps and can help with gentle swaddling if you like.

Before I arrive, open the blinds in your favourite rooms and do a quick tidy on surfaces we will use. Please do not deep clean. Your home is meant to look lived in. A glass of water on the bedside table tells the story as much as the fresh sheets.

You can feed in your favourite chair and change baby on your own change table. ## Will you regret not getting newborn photos

Only you can answer that. Many parents tell me they did not realise how quickly the tiny details would fade from memory. The flaky newborn skin, the fuzz along their shoulders, the way they tuck into you and sigh. Photos make those details easy to revisit, and they become a gift for your child as they grow.

If you are on the fence, a simple in-home session can be a gentle middle ground. No travel, no pressure, just your family as you are.
